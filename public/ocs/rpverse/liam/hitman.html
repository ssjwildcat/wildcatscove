Personality

Unmedicated and without a support system, Liam is unstable. He struggles to show his emotions on his face, often displaying flat effect, but nonetheless feels his emotions strongly. He tends to bottle them up, as to not draw attention to himself, but when the bottle cracks, it’s volatile. He mistrusts and dislikes most people, convinced everyone has it out for him. He rarely keeps relationships, often self-sabotaging the ones he does end up with. He harbors a delusion that nothing he does actually “matters”--something that could easily break if its challenged.

What Liam takes great pains to hide is his desire to hurt those who believes “deserves” it. He’s extremely sensitive to how others treat him and a simple misunderstanding can easily explode into a conspiracy to hurt him in his mind. So he indulges his fears and desire to hurt people with the reasoning that he’s got to hurt people before they hurt him. He’s surprisingly calculating and careful; researching a target for days just to make sure they can’t get the jump on him.

Secretly, Liam wants some happiness in his life—though believes if he takes any opportunity, he will be swiftly punished. He’s more vulnerable than one would expect from a killer—something he is terrified one day will be exploited.

History

Liam refuses to talk about his past but its easily assumed something terrible happened to his family at a young age, leaving him an orphan. His childhood is a mystery but if one could talk to him, they’d find its a sore spot to him and probably contributed to his current identity. In truth, he lost his parents and brother in a house fire at a young age, an experience which he never quite recovered from.

After aging out of the foster system at 18, he’s since drifted through adulthood. His first murder was only somewhat recent; an impulsive attack that he largely got away with out of sheer luck. This fed into his idea that something about his reality was off and that he could do what he liked. So he happily indulged his violent desires; constructing a persona that he could feel comfortable in. He had gotten away with a few murders until he accidentally killed the wrong person; an employee of Mr. Sketches, an elusive and mysterious CEO. Deeply furious of this, Sketches himself tracked him down to deliver an ultimatum: replace the employee and become his personal hitman or suffer dire consequences. Liam, not wanting to suffer a surely terrible and painful punishment, had no choice but to agree.

Skills

If there’s any good to be had in his obsessive nature, it’s that it’s allowed him to self-teach programming and a little engineering work. His prized possession is a modded motorcycle helmet that he obsessively tinkers with. It’s suite of features include—but are not limited to: night vision, a proximity sensor, a signal blocker, etc. The helmet also serves as a separate identity and comfort item.

His preferred weapons of the trade are an axe, a baseball bat and knives. He typically uses the axe along with a knife as backup.
