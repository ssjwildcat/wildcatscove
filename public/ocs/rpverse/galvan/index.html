Galvan used to be a very important creature, having been created by the goddesses of Cyberspace to keep the peace. However, as he was made from their best parts, he grew much more powerful, leading them to fear him. As a result, they locked him away and left him, hoping to forget him. But as their power waned, he’s clawed himself back out and seeks not to lash back out but simply to live as peacefully as he can.

Personality

Power and age has done little to mature Galvan who long ago decided he could do what he likes. And anything he likes is what he does. He wants to be seen as powerful, and in some cases threatening, but doesn’t do anything bigger than petty pranks. He has a bit of an ego and doesn’t see any problem with himself, instead placing himself in the role of a trickster figure. Deep down, he wants to be seen as a good person, a vigilante even. He has a strict moral compass and punishes immoral acts in a sort of “just desserts” way.

While he likes to play pranks, they’re usually only petty as he actually dislikes hurting innocent people. He even dislikes to kill others and only does it if he deems it necessary. He actually like to interact with others as anyone else would and doesn’t like being put on a pedestal, to the point he actively denies his divine status as “The Limbo Prince”.

History

Created in the joint efforts of Konflict and Matka, Galvan, or as he was known “The Limbo Prince” was tasked with bringing peace between the two’s creations. He proved himself exceptional at do this, earning their love and respect by simply living amongst them and showing them how its better to live together in peace. In fact, he almost proved too good as he became more respected and powerful than the goddesses who decided to distance themselves from him.

Galvan noticed their neglect and decided to make an attempt to gain their attention by stealing a silver crown from the vault in their palace. This proved to be the final straw as the goddesses realized he was so powerful he could slip into their home without their knowledge. They lured him to an isolated spot deep in Cyberspace under the guise of wanting to reward him. Instead, they tore open the fabric of Cyberspace and tossed him into the deepest reaches of it, ignoring his wails for help.

Time went on and soon, Galvan found the hold on him had weakened, allowing him to claw himself out of the hole. He found a much different, more modernized Cyberspace where the goddesses had fallen out of favor but legends of him continued. Even though his memories of the time before had faded, he was left with a terror of the goddesses and set out to gain more fame, in case they wanted to hurt him again. Thus, with his mischief, he regained his power as he became something akin to a cryptid or urban legend. While he’s currently afraid of them, its no mistake that if he knew he was much more powerful than them, he’d attempt to kill the goddesses.

Skills

As a deity, Galvan has a massive amount of power in his hands.

He can levitate, teleport and even fly if he wished to. He can manipulate electricity, technology and code to his liking. He can use this to reshape the environment in Cyberspace and create items or constructs.

If harmed, he has a high regeneration factor, able to come back from even the most grievous of wounds. He could change his likeness if he wished but he rather look like his true form.
