A Khajiit through and through, Zandur refuses to give up any of his heritage. Even through a skooma habit, arrest and then the truth of him being Dragonborn, he remains the way he is...for the most part. Some nights, he wonders if his draconic soul has been changing him...or maybe revealing his true self.

Personality

Zandur is not at all what one thinks a hero should be: he’s cranky, foul-mouthed and prone to tempers. However, he has a strong moral compass that compels him to help protect the weak and defenseless. He’s prone to grudges and doesn’t easily forget a slight against him or a perceived innocent. The draconic instinct to dominate bleeds into his personality, moreso since he took the mantle of Dragonborn. This manifests in him being ready to fight anything that threatens him and him getting a little of an ego, especially when he wins at things. Even when he's not trying, he tends to be intimidating. It doesn't help his voice is naturally loud as well.

History

Zandur wasn’t always a jaded person. When he was young, he was eager to learn and a chipper soul. However, this all changed when his father was conscripted into the army of the Aldmeri Dominion because conflict in Hammerfell. When his father perished, Zandur grew jaded, deciding there wasn’t a lot good in the world and growing a deep hatred for the Aldmeri Dominion and the Thalmor by association.

As a teenager, he stayed away from home and his greiving mother. He eventually got caught up in skooma-dealing (and taking), his ventures taking him to northern Cyrodiil. He was here that he was abandoned by his peers when they got caught up in the crossfire between the Empire and the feeling Stormcloaks.

Zandur ended up slated for execution, for no reason other than being in the wrong place at the wrong time. His life was inadvertently saved when Alduin arrived and began to raze Helgen. He escaped and ended up going to Whiterun, running various errands on the way, intent to bring news of the dragon attack. He initially planned to go home after but when he absorbed a dragon soul and was called after the Greybeards, he realized it wasn’t going to be that simple.

Zandur still procrastinated acting on his destiny but was knocked to his sense in an ironic way: a drinking contest gone awry with the Daedric Prince of debauchery Sanguine. He ended up meeting the Greybeards, accepting the mantle of Dragonborn and vowing to defeat Alduin to prevent the end of the world.

The fight with Alduin was no easy one, Zandur only banished the dragon with a final breath of fire after being swatted across the battlefield. By the time he made it back to the Throat of the World, he was well into bleeding out. With quick action by Odaaving who brought him to Whiterun and the Temple of Kynareth, he was saved but asleep for days. Weird dreams haunted him until he finally awoke.

Still recovering and growing weird draconic features, Zandur was attacked by cultists claiming him to be a “false Dragonborn”. Eager to nip this in the bud, he tracked them back to Solstheim where he learned about Miraak. Unfortunately meeting Miraak awakened his draconic instincts to a new ferocity, making him doggedly chase the other Dragonborn—right into Hermaeus Mora’s waiting tentacles. He defeated Miraak but he had the Daedra’s attention now and fled, determined to not bend to Mora’s will.

Zandur spent little time in Skyrim after this, returning to look into things at the College of Winterhold. There he stopped a plot of a Thalmor agent to use the Eye of Magnus for power. He also assisted Arniel Gane in an experiment that ultimately unmade him. Zandur wasn’t too keen (haha) to hold onto the dagger Keening and was grateful, but suspicious, to hand it over to Vashir who came seeking it.

Shortly after this, his sister Kazirra came seeking him and found him, begging him to come home. He was regretful at the pain he caused his family and packed what he wanted and followed her back home.

Skills

As the Dragonborn, his most powerful asset is his ability to use the Thu’um, which is a skill that’s only gotten frighteningly powerful with use. His favored shout is Fire Breath, which he has trained to use in a controlled stream of flame. Other Shouts he favors are Aura Whisper, Unrelenting Force, Cyclone, Dragonrend and Dragon Aspect. Dragon Aspect has the unique ability that with time, he’s honed it to become more dragon-like, with a spined tail and even fully-functioning wings. However, he can only use it for a short amount of time as prolonged use aggravates his draconic instincts.

Magic-wise, Zandur favors Destruction spells, most often fire-based ones. He has a novice’s knowledge in Restoration, something he gained only because he felt it might be helpful.

He prefers swords, typically his Daedric sword which he named “Heartrend”. It has dual enchantments of fire and ice upon it.

Since being declared Dragonborn, he has an uncanny sense of time, being able to tell it accurately even miles underground.

Trivia

He follows the mainstream Riddle’thar religion of the Khajiit but does his best to honor all the deities. He doesn’t see the Divines, or any other of the gods, as the same as his own but distinguishes the Daedra as being part of his pantheon (Azura being Azurah, Sheogorath being the Skooma Cat, etc.) and perceives and treats them as such.

As Dovahkiin, he is in a constant wrestle between his mortal and Dovah sides. The instinct to dominate bleeds into him and he often has to beat it back when it demands he subjugate those below him. Certain things aggravate it, such as wearing the Dragon Priest masks or using Dragon Aspect. Being around Miraak also did to an even higher degree and as a result, a good portion of his time on Solstheim, he was wrestling with himself.

Zandur dislikes the Blades, from when he found Delphine’s note in the tomb of Jurgen Windcaller. He found them disrespectful and having little faith in him, and only followed along them so he could defeat Alduin. When they cut off contact after he refused to slay Paarthurnax, he was happy to never see them again.

Zandur respects the Greybeards, especially their vow for peace. While he does believe a certain degree of violence is needed to achieve peace, he was happy to have them as mentors.

He is close to Paarthurnax, probably more than he knows. He often seeks counsel from him about the constant battle between mortal and Dovah in his soul and values it. He thinks he wouldn’t have been able to keep his mortal values intact without the dragon’s advice. If anyone was to even threaten Paarthurnax, he’d be upon them in no time. The only reason he didn’t kill the Blades was because he was secure in the knowledge only those with the Thu’um could get to him.

Unbeknowst to Zandur, when he was near death after fighting Alduin, he had actually died for a short two minutes. He doesn’t remember anything of the sort when he did but his dreams after often had him looking across Tamriel from atop a tall tower.

Before Zandur uses Fire Breath, smoke tends to leak out of his mouth. If he’s really angry, it may even be flame instead.

Despite being the “Big Bad Dragonborn”, he finds an interest in flowers and books, often collecting both when he’s out on the road.

He jokes about having bad luck, as he is prone to coming upon enemies, including dragons, when he least expects—or wants—it.

He called off skooma after Helgen, having promised he’d give it up if his gods saved him. He kept drinking for a time after but when he got ensnared by Sanguine in the drinking contest, he called that off too. Nowadays, he drinks a lot of coffee and smokes occasionally.

If he was a full-blooded Dovah, his name would be Yolriikaal (Fire-Soul-Champion)
