Once a humble courier, being shot has left Van more than unstable and angry. Revenge is the one thought on his mind as he beelines it to Vegas. But once that’s done, what is there to do? Maybe, it’s time to shake things up in the Mojave.

Personality

On the outset, Van seems a cheerful and bombastic man with unflappable confidence in himself. However, in time, one would find his brain injury has left him wild and unstable. He’s unpredictable and prone to wild mood swings and fits of violence. He’s impulsive but is surprisingly cunning and wily with a silver tongue that allows him to trick others. He’s more clever than he looks and is not above manipulating others to get what he wants. He often speaks in sarcasm and snark, coming off as more hostile than he really is.

If you do count yourself as one of his friends, you’ll find a loyal and determined ally that will do anything for you. If you are unlucky enough to be deemed an “asset” to him, you’ll find him helpful up until he turns the tables on you with no lack of glee. His enemies find him manic and downright terrifying, resorting to violence with no lack of excitement. Despite all this, he has a fondness for the underdog and the downtrodden, willing to go to bat for them and this being his primary motivation. He’s accepting of people from all walks of life, even the likes of super mutants and robots. While he is an uncannily sociable person, he doesn’t mind being alone and even sometimes craves it. He doesn’t tend to show sensitive emotions, often trying to work through them himself before he turns to others.

History

A lot of Van’s history has been lost on him, as he suffered from near total amnesia after being shot in the head. What he does know is he’s a well-traveled courier who spent a lot of time in the west and is notorious for taking the jobs no one else would, including making the Divide run multiple times (and accidentally causing its destruction in the form of making a delivery of a detonator there).

Upon waking up in Goodsprings after being robbed, shot and left for dead, Van’s only thought was revenge and once cleared for travel, set out to do just that. His travels to New Vegas left him with little love for both the NCR and Legion. Once at the Strip and having met with Mr. House, he found he didn’t like the man either. After enacting his revenge on Benny, retrieving the Platinum Chip and meeting Yes Man did Van find his motivation: use Yes Man’s assistance to assume control of the region and turn it over to the various communities wherein.

With his plans set up and plenty of detours along the way, Van was able to play his hand at the Second Battle of Hoover Dam, utterly crushing the Legion and ousting the NCR from a newly independent Mojave.

Skills

Van fancies himself an all-purposes gunslinger, being skilled in both guns and energy weapons, though he prefers good ol’ traditional guns. He’s good at sneaking about and getting into places he’s not wanted. He’s good with his words and able to get what he wants most of the time with nothing but his words. He’s no stranger to manipulation, using it to get what he wants and achieve his ends. As a courier, he’s agile and able to move fast over all terrain. He also is knowledgeable in surviving in the wastes, as he’s spent long stretches of time there.

His arsenal consists of an anti-materiel rifle he’s dubbed “Clarabelle”, Medicine Stick, a modified Ranger Sequoia, and a switchblade.

Trivia

Due to his brain injury, Van has lost the vast majority of his long-term memories, being only able to recall snatches of it. He also has problems with his short-term memory, using his Pip-Boy to take notes of things he wants to remember.

He sometimes get migraines and isn’t above using chems for the pain, particularly Med-X.

A nickname he’s gained is “The Ghost of the Mojave” due to his stark white hair and the fact he seemingly “returned to life”.

He doesn’t like trains because the loud noises they make give him migraines.

Van doesn’t actually know the day he was born so he chose the day he woke up after being shot as his birthday.

He prefers Sunset Sarsaparilla to Nuka-Cola.

He thinks robots are neat and is typically able to understand them, despite no apparent reason for him to be able to.

He can’t sing at all and is unable to hold a tune, which is unfortunate for a man who has what is effectively a portable radio strapped to his wrist at most times.

After the Sierra Madre, tight things around his neck and the sound of beeping sends him into a wild panic.

His preferred way of working through things is disappearing into the desert for days at a time.

Doc Mitchell gave him the two 9mm bullets he pulled out of his head so Van kept them and put them on a leather cord.

He painted the spade on the back of his duster after returning from the Divide and meeting Ulysses.
