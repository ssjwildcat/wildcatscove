Moon-Dancer is a person who often struggles with her purpose. As terrible as it for everyone else, the Oblivion Crisis has given her one: help Martin Septim close shut the jaws of Oblivion and help him ascend to the throne of the Imperial Empire. But what happens to someone who is so dependent of their purpose when its suddenly robbed from them, along with the person they loved the most?

Personality

Moon-Dancer comes off as humble yet excitable, naive and sincere. She tends to switch rapidly between trusting people at their word and mistrusting everything someone tells her. Truthfully, she constantly fights extreme mood swings and volatile thoughts. She can be somewhat of a people-pleaser, as she is extremely concerned with making sure people like her and don’t want to hurt her. She’s somewhat selfish, as she considers her own feelings and safety first. She struggles to interact with people, as she overthinks how they might feel about her. She gets attached easily to people she feels understand her and this has resulted in a codependent attachment to Martin. She finds change difficult to adapt to and can easily collapse into depression after drastic changes in her life.

History

Moon-Dancer’s birth family was neglectful of her, which instilled into her the idea she can’t rely on anyone but herself. She ran away from home as a teenager and joined a temple a few villages over, intent not to worship but to learn their Claw-Dances to defend herself. After a few years, she joined a caravan network traveling Elsweyr and the nearby provinces.

In Cyrodiil, some years after, she accidentally killed a man who was harassing her caravan and was promptly sent to the Imperial Prison. While she had been there for a few weeks, she was moved to the cell containing the escape route only the night before the Emperor died. As in canon, she escaped when the Emperor was being led out of the city, heard his last words and received the Amulet of Kings. She ferried it immediately to Jauffre and then to find Martin, who she bonded with immediately on their shared complex relationship with the gods.

She happily helped Martin the entire way, growing a queerplatonic and codependent relationship with him that was mutual (though he recognized, to some extent, how dependent she was on him). In her downtime, she would either rove around helping people, doing Fighter’s Guild jobs or scaling the ranks of the Mage’s Guild.

Unfortunately, when Mehrunes Dagon invaded and Martin subsequently sacrificed himself to banish him, Moon-Dancer was shattered. In the immediate aftermath, Jauffre attempted to comfort her but she attacked him, only able to pin the blame on all this on him for losing the amulet in the first place. She came short of mauling him to death before she came to, realized what she had done and bolted, escaping into the wilderness thanks to the confusion. It was in this desperate, suicidal state she found the door in the Niben Bay.

In the Shivering Isles, she found herself going to Sheogorath who gave her a tempting proposal: if she hurt so much being Moon-Dancer, why doesn’t she just...become someone else? So she ended up helping him, not because she cared about the plight of the Shivering Isles but because she thought it would purge her of her pain. And as a result, her fate as the future Madgod was sealed.

Skills

Moon-Dancer is adept at Claw-Dances, the Khajiiti form of martial arts, having trained with it for some years. She also uses Destruction and Illusion magic proficiently, the former for defense and the latter to help her lackluster social skills.

She also loves to sew and make jewelry and often makes simple pieces in her downtime.

More References


Martin Septim

Moon hit it off with Martin right away and, feeling bereft of purpose, immediately threw herself into his cause. The pair grew close with both confiding in each other in such a time of crisis. In a way, Moon fell in love with Martin, planning on devoting herself to him as his confidant and bodyguard when he became Emperor. Unfortunately, when Martin sacrificed himself, it broke her.

Sheogorath

Moon was undoubtedly preyed upon by the Daedra in her most vulnerable time. In a sense, she understood this but followed him anyway. His promise that helping him stop the Greymarch by mantling him would make the pain go away was just too tantalizing.

Trivia

She likes to collect Welkynd Stones; not to use but because she thinks they’re pretty.

Moon-Dancer’s Amulet of Akatosh is a gift from Martin that was once his own. She ended up giving him a woven bracelet with beads and metal rings. It’s deeply sentimental to her but, as it is a depiction of Akatosh, becomes something she feels complicated about Post-Oblivion Crisis. Sometimes, after, she would talk to it as if it was Akatosh.

Moon-Dancer made her outfit with the vision of her one day being the body guard to Martin as the Emperor; thus she incorporated a purple/red/gold color scheme, red diamonds and some Cyrodiilic flourishes. It’s only by sheer coincidence it also shares a color scheme with Sheogorath...

Sometimes, when she’s feeling really panicked, she casts an Invisibility spell and can’t become visible again until she calms down.
