Plagued by nightmares and disconnected from his people, Vashir had always felt restless and like he didn’t quite belong wherever he was. His restlessness eventually led to him coming to Cyrodiil where he’d be arrested and sent to Morrowind, which started to seem too familiar to the Khajiit...

Personality

He’s got a cocky streak but whether it’s for real or just for show, depends on the day. He’s terribly stubborn and has problems calling it quits. He’s tenacious and willing to set himself to what he feels must be done—perhaps to a detrimental degree. When relaxed, he has a sarcastic and light-hearted streak, trying to not take things that don’t need to be taken seriously seriously. He’s non-violent and prefers to use methods like subterfuge or his words to get out of conflict but is extremely talented at defending himself if need be. He’s good at “switching masks” and presenting himself to get what he needs from someone. As a result, he tends to be mistrustful of others and always wary of others’ true intentions. He tends to brood, often even blaming himself for things he couldn’t help. He’s got a bit of a hero complex, feeling like he’s letting others down if he’s living up to his role as hero.

History

Pre-Morrowind

Vashir grew up an orphan in a small village in Pelletine, after the disappearance of his parents. As a result, he grew up in the care of the Clan Mother and wider community. He always felt off...different. He dreamed of nightmarish visons of red skies, of battles between elves, of a golden elven figure. He didn’t feel like he quite belonged.

As a teenager, he grew restless enough to leave, joining a caravan to Cyrodiil where he stayed and joined the Thieves’ Guild there. He was happy for a time. But it all ended when, due to a lack of sleep and concentration, he was caught mid-heist, beat half-to death and then sent to the Imperial Prison. After a few too long months, he was shipped off to Morrowind to his great confusion.

Morrowind

In Morrowind, Vashir initially struggled to adjust in a land of people who didn’t like him and an environment that seemed keen on killing him. With nowhere to go and feeling pressured, he found no choice but to help Caius Cosades and his Blades investigate the prophecies of the Nerevarine...prophecies that seemed to sung to his desire to belong. He grew excited at the idea of being the hero...until he caught corprus in the caverns of Illunibi. He trekked to Tel Fyr, got “cured” and, with the thought that he had no other choice, set to fulfilling the prophecy.

Being bequeathed Moon-and-Star by Azura only gave him a heavier responsibility but Vashir prevailed, uniting the Tribes and Great Houses, meeting Vivec and retrieving the Profane Tools before marching to Red Mountain. Under the mountain came a desperate struggle between him and Dagoth Ur. When the time came to banish the Heart, Vashir hesitated...a choice that cost him dearly when Dagoth Ur attacked him viciously, tearing out one of his eyes and severely burning his face. Somehow, however, through the pain, he managed to make a last sprint to the Heart and do away with it. It brought him relief and an odd sort of melancholy.

Tribunal & Bloodmoon

Afterwards, Vashir settled back in Balmora, intent to recover and access where he should go from here. But this hope was dashed when he found himself targeted by the Dark Brotherhood. Some investigation sent him to the city of Mournhold where he found himself stuck between two factions; the King and the Temple.

Stuck for the time being, Vashir helped out best he could, leaning towards helping Almalexia, who he felt some kind of obligation too though found unsettling, due to Nerevar’s past relationship to her. After a while though, he found his feelings about her validated as she bid him to help her with cruel actions, such as bringing ash storms to Mournhold. His anxieties became real when, after journeying through the Clockwork City and finding the long-dead body of Sotha Sil, he was confronted by the goddess.

A long, hard fight was fought but at the end, Vashir won, standing over the fatally wounded Almalexia. It was then something broke in him, more than a year of stress compounding and collapsing after he did something he said he wouldn’t: actually killing the Tribunal and further crystallizing his identity as Nerevar’s incarnation. Terrified, hurt and confused, he fled back to Mournhold.

Vashir’s breakdown made him flee to where he felt the gods couldn’t find him: Solstheim. But cold weather effectively stranded him once he realized what exactly he had done. And soon enough he found he had just stumbled into another prophecy: the Bloodmoon Prophecy. The signs culminated into him being kidnapped one night by werewolves, brought to a glacier and being forced to participate in the Hunt of Hircine.

Together with the captain from the Imperial fort on the island, the pair got through the glacier, slaying werewolves and the other participants in the Hunt. At the end though it was just Vashir again the frost giant Karstaag and beyond, Hircine himself. After a short but intense fight, the pair locked together, Trueflame in Hircine and Hircine’s antler in Vashir’s shoulder. After a few moments, Hircine conceded victory.

Post-Morrowind

Vashir left Solstheim to wander the mainland of Morrowind until he decided to return to his home of Elsweyr. He stayed there for some time...until the end of the Third Era, where he was called to Vivec City. He experienced nightmares of red skies on his way there and once there, he found out why he was called: Vivec’s godhood was waning and he wanted to leave Morrowind. Hesitant, Vashir agreed to take him with him back to Elsweyr...where they ended up right in time for the Oblivion Crisis. For until it was over, the pair traveled all over the province, helping out where they could.

Right at the beginning of the Fourth Era, Vashir had to contend with the consequence of Dagoth Ur’s vanquishing: the Red Year, an event that ultimately made him renounce Azura. Many years later, a burglar robbing him got a hold of Keening, which sent him on a journey to retrieve it, sending him to Skyrim where he received it back from the Last Dragonborn Zandur.

Now, Vashir has traveled to Solstheim, curious as to how the place changed. He’s ended up sticking around, helping Neloth investigate the ash spawn and the Heart Stones.

Skills

Vashir’s a natural jack-of-all-trades, being able to pick up just about any weapon and figure it out in no time. He prefers blades like his daggers (which he keeps at least two on his person). His favorite weapon however is the sword Trueflame. He’s also partial to spears and was in possession of the Spear of Bitter Mercy for a time.

He’s naturally stealthy and getting into places without being seen. He’s good at lockpicking and preferring doing it manually over a spell. He’s proficient in passive magic, such as invisibility and calming spells. He knows a few other spells, like Healing or minor Destruction spells in a pinch.

As a hobby, he likes to carve, especially as a way to calm or ground himself. He likes to pick up skills and figures if he’s been blessed with eternal youth, he might as well use it to learn.

Trivia

Vashir isn’t aware if he’s a true reincarnation of Nerevar but suspects something more is going on. He has feelings about Morrowind he can’t explain, like feeling pity for Dagoth Ur despite feeling terrified of him. He can pick up most weapons and know how to innately use them. He has memories and dreams of things he knows aren’t his memories. Even being on Vvardenfell gives him a sense of deja vu.

Due to Vashir’s infection and subsequent curing, he retains the positive effects of the Corpus disease; increased strength and endurance, immunity to illness and eternal youth.

He has chronic nightmares and sleeps rarely as a result. His dreams sometimes contain memories that aren’t his own or prophetic hints of the future.

Vashir finds it difficult to speak the Khajiiti dialect. While he can do it, he has to focus on it. He’s most comfortable in the Cyrodiilic dialect, due to his time in Cyrodiil and Vvardenfell.

He likes to sing and has a high airy voice. However, he doesn’t often.
